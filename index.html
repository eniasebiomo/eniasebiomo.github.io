Autodesk Internship

Applied Innovation in the Office of the CTO

Last summer I spent 10 weeks at Autodesk, between their main office at 1 Market St. and the amazing Workshop at Pier 9. During that summer I along with two other interns, Connor and Ali were tasked with "breaking Fusion 360" - Autodesk's cloud-based CAD/CAM software. In other words we were given free reign to design projects that would would push Fusion to its limits and then head down to Pier 9 to fabricate them. Throughout the internship we reported directly to CEO Carl Bass and Product Managers from the Fusion 360 Team.

LeviLamp

For our first project we spent two weeks prototyping a lamp with a levitating lampshade. Here is the initial render and the final model. We spelled out 'FLOAT' replacing the 'A' with the Autodesk logo.


We used a combination of the Fortus 450mc and Objet Connex 350 to 3D print the structure of the lamp out of black ABS-like material and translucent VeroClear respectively, 76 NeoPixel LEDs to light it up, an Arduino Metro Mini and Bluetooth LE chip to connect to it and finally installed an electromagnetic module to provide the levitation effect.


OCTOPod

Later in the summer, we spent 6 weeks designing and fabricating OCTOPod - a 3D printed robotic Octopus.



We fully designed OctoPod in Fusion 360 and prototyped it, then headed to the shop.

The tentacles, head and inner body were all printed out of black ABS-like material on the Fortus, the eyes out of translucent VeroClear on the Objet and the tentacles were printed in PLA SemiFlex on a makerbot to give it the wiggling effect. The tentacles were given movement by 16 Herkulex servo motors controlled by a Teensy 3.2 and Bluefruit LE chip - all powered by a 7.4v 8000mAh LiPo battery.


I even got the opportunity to interview the CEO Carl Bass in front of an audience of 100 employees and broadcast live to the company globally during one of the monthly 'Carl Talks'.


All in all, it was a solid summer in San Francisco, and I learned a lot at Autodesk.

Twitter Software Engineering Internship

The summer after my freshmen year, I interned at Twitter on the Direct Messages Team, working on fronteb web development. My task was to increase engagement in DMs by making them more exciting. After a variety of prototypes, my final project centered around super-sized emojis on Web that produced exciting effects on hover, as well as an 'emoji bar' that allows you to select from the top few most popular emojis and enter them directly into the composer. My team ended up continuing what I started on Android and iOS and shipping my work to production - it's now being utilized by millions of twitter users!
